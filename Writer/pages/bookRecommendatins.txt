Here’s a list of 10 impactful books that every student might benefit from reading during their life. These books offer a mix of personal growth, critical thinking, creativity, and global perspective:

"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee - A timeless classic about empathy, justice, and human morality.

"The Alchemist" by Paulo Coelho - An inspiring tale about following one’s dreams and discovering one’s true purpose.

"Man’s Search for Meaning" by Viktor E. Frankl - A profound reflection on finding purpose, even in challenging circumstances.

"1984" by George Orwell - A gripping novel exploring themes of individuality, freedom, and societal control.

"Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" by Yuval Noah Harari - A fascinating exploration of human history, culture, and evolution.

"The Diary of a Young Girl" by Anne Frank - An intimate, moving account of hope and resilience during difficult times.

"Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman - A deep dive into the complexities of the human mind and decision-making.

"The Power of Now" by Eckhart Tolle - A guide to mindfulness, presence, and living a fulfilling life.

"Atomic Habits" by James Clear - Practical strategies for building good habits and breaking bad ones.

"The Gita" (Bhagavad Gita) - A spiritual and philosophical masterpiece offering timeless wisdom.
